Tour Overview

Tanzania's Complete Northern Circuit

This is Tanzania done properly. Seven days, five destinations, and a route that has been refined over decades of guiding to capture each park at its most extraordinary — not just its most famous. The Northern Circuit is the most celebrated safari itinerary in Africa for good reason: it combines the intimate, elephant-dense forests of Tarangire, the surreal flamingo shores of Lake Natron (still Tanzania's best-kept secret), the sheer scale of the Serengeti's endless plains, the tree-climbing lions and Rift Valley grandeur of Lake Manyara, and the geological wonder of Ngorongoro Crater — the world's largest intact volcanic caldera and home to the highest density of wildlife in Africa.

The sequence matters. We begin in Tarangire — dry, baobab-studded, and game-rich — to calibrate your eye before the wider landscapes ahead. Lake Natron is timed for the late afternoon light that turns the soda lake a deep rose. The Serengeti is given two full days, not one. Manyara is the gentle reintroduction to forest after open plains. And Ngorongoro closes the circuit with an encounter that leaves every visitor speechless: three thousand animals in a single volcanic bowl, nowhere to go, and nowhere to be but here.

Tour Highlights

Five Parks, One Epic Journey

🐘Tarangire's Elephant Herds: Africa's highest concentration of elephants per square kilometre outside Botswana. Family groups of forty or more move between the ancient baobabs — an encounter unlike anywhere else in Tanzania.
🦩Lake Natron's Flamingo Nursery: The world's only breeding ground for East Africa's 2.5 million lesser flamingos. The soda lake turns scarlet in afternoon light; the landscape feels like another planet entirely.
🐆Serengeti — Africa's Greatest Stage: Two full days in the Serengeti, covering the central Seronera valley for year-round game and the riverine forest where leopards drape themselves over branches above the Seronera River.
🦁Lake Manyara's Tree Lions: One of the world's only populations of habitual tree-climbing lions, a behaviour that still puzzles researchers. Finding a pride elevated in an acacia is among Tanzania's most singular wildlife experiences.
🌋Ngorongoro Crater: A full descent into the 260-square-kilometre caldera floor, home to the Big Five including Africa's last concentrations of black rhino, lion prides, and flocks of flamingos on the soda lake at the crater's heart.
🌅Rift Valley Panoramas: The entire circuit runs along or across the Great Rift Valley. Sundowners on the crater rim, dawns on the Serengeti plains, and the escarpment views from Manyara — the light here does something to every photograph.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Seven Days Across Northern Tanzania

1
Day

Arusha to Tarangire — Baobabs & the Elephant Kingdom

📍 Tarangire National Park

Morning

Departure from your Arusha hotel at 07:00. The drive south passes through farmland and the town of Makuyuni before the landscape opens into the dry thornbush country that announces Tarangire. The change is immediate and dramatic: within minutes of the park gate, the baobab trees begin — enormous, ancient, impossible-looking things that can live for two thousand years and hold thousands of litres of water in their swollen trunks. En route, your guide briefs you on the park's ecology and what to expect in each habitat zone.

Afternoon

Enter the park and begin the main game drive along the Tarangire River, the park's lifeblood. In the dry season, every animal in the ecosystem is drawn here: elephant herds of thirty, forty, fifty individuals moving in slow procession; zebra and wildebeest crossing the river shallows; impala rams sparring in the riverine acacia. Tarangire is also exceptional for tree-climbing python sightings and has one of Tanzania's finest populations of fringe-eared oryx. Picnic lunch is served beneath a sausage tree beside the river.

Evening

Late afternoon drive through the open grasslands east of the river — the golden-hour light over the baobab silhouettes is one of Tanzania's most-photographed scenes, and for good reason. Check in at your lodge as the sky turns. Sundowner drinks on the terrace as the park falls quiet below.

🌿 Night at Tarangire Sopa Lodge or Oliver's Camp (by tier)
2
Day

Tarangire Full Day — Swamps, Predators & the Northern Wilderness

📍 Tarangire National Park

Early Morning

A 06:00 departure catches the park at its most active. The Silale Swamp in the park's south — one of Tarangire's lesser-visited sections — holds enormous concentrations of buffalo, elephant, and waterbuck in the early morning. Lion prides patrol the swamp edge at dawn. This is also the best area for the park's significant cheetah population, which uses the open grasslands beyond the swamp for hunting.

Mid-Morning to Afternoon

A bush breakfast is served in the field — the table laid in an open clearing while elephants move at a respectful distance — before the drive continues north through the park's central areas. The afternoon provides a second pass along the Tarangire River as herds build in intensity toward dusk. This is Tarangire at its most overwhelming: vehicles can sometimes count over four hundred elephants in a single afternoon's drive.

Evening

Return to camp as the park closes. Optional night game drive available for guests at certain lodges — an entirely different experience with spring hares, genets, and the occasional serval catching the spotlight. Dinner and debrief with your guide as you review the day's sightings and plan the Lake Natron journey for tomorrow.

🌿 Second Night at Tarangire Lodge
3
Day

Lake Natron — The Flamingo Planet

📍 Lake Natron, Rift Valley

Morning

Depart early — the drive to Lake Natron takes approximately three hours through increasingly dramatic scenery. The road passes through Maasai country: red-ochre warriors moving cattle across the dusty plains, bomas visible at intervals, the world becoming more remote and more extraordinary with every kilometre. The Rift Valley escarpment closes in as you descend toward the lake. Ol Doinyo Lengai — the Maasai's "Mountain of God," one of Africa's only active carbonatite volcanoes — comes into view as a perfect cone rising from the valley floor.

Afternoon

Arrive at Lake Natron by midday. The lake is a natural wonder that should not exist: its waters are more alkaline than bleach, reaching temperatures of 60°C in summer, and yet it sustains the world's only major breeding colony for lesser flamingos — up to 2.5 million birds nesting on the lake's caustic mudflats. The afternoon light turns the soda lake's surface a deep crimson and rose pink, depending on the algae levels. Optional guided walk along the lake shore — close enough to hear the flamingos, far enough not to disturb them.

Evening

Sundowners with a direct view of Ol Doinyo Lengai and the lake reflecting the last light. For those with energy: a walk to the nearby Engare Sero waterfall, a freshwater cascade into the Rift Valley that offers an extraordinary contrast to the hostile lake behind it. Dinner under an open sky with one of Tanzania's least light-polluted horizons above.

🌋 Night at Lake Natron Tented Camp
4
Day

Natron to Serengeti — Crossing into the Endless Plains

📍 Serengeti National Park

Morning

An early departure gives time for a final dawn walk beside the lake before the drive west toward the Serengeti. The route passes through some of Tanzania's most remote country — the Ngorongoro Highlands, the crater rim visible to the south, and then the long descent onto the Serengeti plains. The moment the park boundary becomes the horizon is a moment every first-time visitor remembers: the land simply opens, and opens, and opens. There is no preparation for the Serengeti's scale.

Afternoon

Enter the Serengeti through the Naabi Hill Gate and begin the afternoon game drive through the southern plains — the calving grounds of the Great Migration. Depending on season, wildebeest herds may be visible on the horizon in numbers that defy comprehension. The central Seronera Valley, where the Seronera and Orangi rivers converge, is the park's year-round wildlife core. Lion prides, leopard, cheetah, and elephant are reliably present. Picnic lunch is taken at a viewpoint above the river.

Evening

Arrive at your lodge in the Seronera area as the light fades. The Serengeti's sunsets — flat horizon, no obstruction, the sky's full range of colour — are unlike anywhere else. Dinner at camp.

🐆 Night at Serengeti Sopa Lodge or Serengeti Migration Camp (by tier)
5
Day

Full Day in the Serengeti — Big Cats & the Migration

📍 Serengeti National Park

Dawn Drive

A 05:30 departure — while the air is still cold and the grass still silver with dew — is the Serengeti's finest hour. Nocturnal predators are finishing their night's work; leopards carry kills into the trees before dawn breaks. The Seronera River's riverine forest is the most reliable leopard habitat in East Africa, and morning is the hour to find them. Your guide knows each individual territory. Two hours of pre-sunrise game viewing can produce encounters that define an entire trip.

Morning to Midday

Continue through the Seronera Valley and onto the open plains, following the current position of the wildebeest herds (which your guide will have tracked the evening before). The full-day format allows the flexibility to follow a cheetah hunt, stay with a lion pride through a kill, or simply drive slowly through a plain of a hundred thousand wildebeest — experiences that two-day Serengeti visitors miss entirely. A full bush lunch is served in the field.

Afternoon

The afternoon drive is dedicated to the river crossings if timing aligns with the Migration (July–October), or to the kopjes — the ancient granite outcrops scattered across the Serengeti plains — which function as lion kingdom headquarters and offer spectacular elevated photography. The Moru Kopjes in the south hold a population of black rhino that can sometimes be seen at dusk. Return to camp for the final night on the Serengeti plains.

🐆 Second Night at Serengeti Lodge
6
Day

Serengeti to Lake Manyara — Forest, Flamingos & Tree Lions

📍 Lake Manyara National Park

Morning

A final early drive in the Serengeti before departing east through the Ngorongoro Conservation Area and descending to Lake Manyara. The conservation area drive passes through Maasai villages and highland forests — a complete change from the open plains — before the Rift Valley escarpment comes into view and Lake Manyara appears below, silver-blue and vast, its near shore already pink with flamingos.

Afternoon

Enter Lake Manyara National Park for an afternoon game drive. After the Serengeti's scale, Manyara's intimacy is a deliberate contrast: the park is compact, forested, and rewards patience and close observation. The groundwater forest along the park's northern section is the habitat of the tree-climbing lions — the guides know which trees, which prides, and which time of day. The forest also holds olive baboon troops, blue monkeys, and the secretive bushbuck.

Evening

Emerge from the forest to drive along the lake shore at golden hour — the flamingo shallows catching the last light, the escarpment wall glowing above. Check into your lodge on the crater rim with views across the lake below. Dinner on the terrace — this evening, the circuit feels close to complete.

🦁 Night at Lake Manyara Serena Lodge or Kirurumu Tented Lodge (by tier)
7
Day

Ngorongoro Crater — The World's Greatest Caldera

📍 Ngorongoro Conservation Area

Early Morning

Depart by 06:30 for the Ngorongoro Crater descent. The crater rim sits at 2,300 metres; the descent to the floor takes approximately 45 minutes on a steep volcanic track. As the vehicle drops through the morning cloud, the crater floor reveals itself in layers — first the grasslands, then the soda lake at the centre, then the dots of wildebeest, the moving specks of zebra, the distant smear of flamingos. The caldera is 260 square kilometres and contains approximately 25,000 large animals that cannot leave. They have been here, self-sustaining, since the volcano collapsed 2.5 million years ago.

Morning Drive

The morning game drive prioritises the black rhino — Ngorongoro holds one of Africa's last stable populations — and the crater's resident lion prides, which are the most genetically isolated in Africa and extraordinarily relaxed around vehicles. The central soda lake at Ngorongoro's heart holds flamingos and pelicans; hippo pools at the Ngoitokitok Springs are a reliable stop. Your guide will also look for the leopards that hunt the forest patches along the crater's inner walls.

Midday

Picnic lunch at the designated area beside the Ngoitokitok Springs — the most atmospherically located picnic spot in Tanzania, with a hippo pool five metres away and Maasai cattle on the crater rim 600 metres above your head. A final loop through the crater floor before the ascent.

Afternoon

Ascend the crater and drive back to Arusha, arriving approximately 16:30–17:30. Drop-off at your hotel or Kilimanjaro International Airport. The seven days of Tanzania's Northern Circuit, complete. Optional extension available: continue to Zanzibar for beach days — the most popular post-safari addition.

June–October is peak season: the long dry season brings the Great Migration's northern river crossings at the Mara River (July–October), superb game viewing throughout with short grass giving maximum visibility, and the best flamingo numbers at Lake Natron. January–February offers the Migration's calving season on the southern Serengeti plains — thousands of wildebeest calves born each day, and the predator activity that follows. November–December brings the short rains and a dramatic lush green landscape with far fewer tourists — recommended for photographers and those seeking a quieter experience. March–May sees the long rains; some tracks become difficult but all parks remain open, and rates drop significantly.

July–October: the Mara River crossings in the north — the most dramatic spectacle, with crocodiles taking wildebeest as they cross. November–December: the herds return south.
